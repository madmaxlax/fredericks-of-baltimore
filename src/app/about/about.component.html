35+ Years of Collecting and "Picking"

Fred has amassed a collection that, at one point, filled multiple warehouses from his career in construction.

Fred grew his collection being a partner at Struever Brothers.
Struever Brothers Eccles & Rouse was a development company founded in 1974 by Bill Struever, alongside his brother Fred, college roommate Cobber Eccles, and Ted Rouse (son of renowned developer James Rouse), primarily focused on revitalizing urban areas, particularly in Baltimore, Maryland, by repurposing old industrial buildings and brownfield sites into mixed-use developments; known for their significant contributions to community revitalization through large-scale urban redevelopment projects, often utilizing historic preservation and sustainable practices.

Fred’s role evolved from construction management to be the ‘picker’ for job sites; going in and tagging goods and materials that were worth saving and often reincorporating into the new development. With a saying that ‘two of anything is a collection,’ the amount quickly grew and at one point filled warehouses. Now, the remaining items live at Frederick’s of Baltimore with almost all still available for sale or re-use into new artwork.
While SBE&R sadly did not survive the 2008 Financial Crisis, aspects of the company live on with Bill and Cobber at Cross Street Partners.
